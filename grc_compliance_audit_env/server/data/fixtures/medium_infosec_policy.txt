1. DATA CLASSIFICATION AND INFORMATION HANDLING
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
1.1 Classification Scheme

All information assets owned, processed, or transmitted by Nexus Financial
Services (hereinafter "the Organisation") must be classified into one of the
following four categories based on sensitivity and business impact:

  CONFIDENTIAL: Highly sensitive data whose unauthorised disclosure could cause
  significant harm to the Organisation, clients, or regulators. Examples include
  customer financial records, personally identifiable information (PII), trade
  secrets, and board communications. Access is restricted to named individuals
  with explicit management approval.

  INTERNAL USE ONLY: Information intended for internal business operations and
  not for external disclosure. Examples include internal HR records, project
  plans, and operational process documents. Access is restricted to employees
  and contractors with a legitimate business need.

  PUBLIC: Information approved for release to the general public, including
  marketing materials, published regulatory disclosures, and press releases.
  No access restrictions apply.

  RESTRICTED (REGULATORY): Information subject to explicit regulatory handling
  requirements, including RBI mandates, SEBI filings, and customer data governed
  by PDPA. Special handling, encryption, and audit requirements apply.

1.2 Labelling and Handling

All documents and data assets must be clearly labelled with their classification
level. Confidential and Restricted data must be encrypted in transit using
TLS 1.2 or higher. Physical documents classified Confidential must be stored in
locked cabinets and shredded when no longer required. Electronic media containing
Confidential data must be securely disposed of using industry-standard data
wiping tools.

1.3 Data Retention

Data is retained for the minimum period required by business necessity and
applicable regulations. Upon expiry of the retention period, data must be
securely deleted or anonymised in accordance with the Organisation's Data
Retention Schedule.

2. ACCESS CONTROL
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 Access Principles

Access to all information systems and assets of the Organisation is governed by
the principles of least privilege and need-to-know. Users are granted only the
minimum level of access required to perform their assigned job functions.

2.2 User Account Lifecycle

  a) Provisioning: New user accounts are created only upon receipt of an
     approved Access Request Form (ARF) signed by the line manager and
     countersigned by the system owner. User accounts are provisioned to
     the approved role profile in the identity management system.

  b) Regular Review: All user access rights are reviewed semi-annually by
     system owners. Dormant accounts — those with no login activity for more
     than 90 days — are disabled automatically by the identity management
     platform.

  c) Deprovisioning: Upon employee termination or contract expiry, all access
     rights are revoked within four hours of HR notification. Line managers
     are responsible for notifying IT and HR simultaneously on the employee's
     last working day.

2.3 Privileged Access

Administrative and privileged access is restricted to authorised IT personnel
only. All privileged accounts are registered in the Privileged Access Management
(PAM) system. Privileged access sessions are recorded and retained for audit
purposes for a minimum of one year.

2.4 Remote Access

All remote connections to Organisation systems must be made via an approved
Virtual Private Network (VPN). Remote access is restricted to Organisation-issued
devices only.

NOTE TO AUDITOR: This section does not currently specify a multi-factor
authentication (MFA) requirement for any access category, including privileged
access. This is an identified gap requiring policy amendment before the upcoming
ISO 27001 certification audit.

3. INCIDENT RESPONSE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 Scope and Objectives

This section establishes the Organisation's approach to detecting, reporting,
and managing information security incidents. An information security incident
is defined as any event that compromises the confidentiality, integrity, or
availability of information assets.

3.2 Incident Categorisation

Incidents are categorised by severity to determine the appropriate escalation
and response timeline:

  P1 CRITICAL: Active breach, ransomware, data exfiltration, or system
               compromise affecting customer data. Response within 30 minutes.

  P2 HIGH:     Confirmed malware infection, unauthorised access, or service
               outage. Response within 2 hours.

  P3 MEDIUM:   Suspected security event, policy violation, or failed access
               attempt. Response within 8 hours.

  P4 LOW:      Minor anomaly, informational event, or procedural deviation.
               Response within 48 hours.

3.3 Reporting Procedure

All employees must report suspected security incidents immediately to the
Security Operations Centre (SOC) via the dedicated incident hotline
(+91-22-XXXX-XXXX) or the incident reporting portal. Incidents must not be
disclosed externally without explicit approval from the CISO or Legal Team.

3.4 Incident Response Plan

The Organisation maintains a documented Incident Response Plan (IRP) that
defines roles, responsibilities, communication trees, and escalation paths for
each severity level. The IRP is tested via annual tabletop exercises. The CISO
is the Incident Response Owner.

3.5 Post-Incident Review

Following resolution of any P1 or P2 incident, a Post-Incident Review (PIR)
must be completed within five business days. The PIR documents root cause,
impact assessment, evidence collected, and corrective actions taken.

NOTE TO AUDITOR: This section does not currently define a vulnerability
scanning or patch management process. Identification and remediation of
technical vulnerabilities requires a dedicated sub-section or standalone
Vulnerability Management Policy. This is an identified gap.

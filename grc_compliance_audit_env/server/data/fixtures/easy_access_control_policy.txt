ACCESS CONTROL POLICY v1.0
Organisation: Acme Technologies Ltd
Classification: Internal Use Only
Effective Date: 01 January 2025
Review Cycle: Annual

================================================================================

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
This policy establishes the requirements and responsibilities for controlling
access to information systems, applications, networks, and data assets owned or
managed by Acme Technologies Ltd (hereinafter "the Organisation"). It applies to
all employees, contractors, consultants, temporary staff, and third-party
personnel who are granted access to organisational information assets.

The Organisation is committed to governing information security through a formal,
documented policy framework aligned with recognised international standards. All
personnel with access to information assets are accountable for adhering to
their assigned roles and responsibilities as defined by the Information Security
Management function. Management direction is provided through the Information
Security Steering Committee, which approves this policy and ensures it is
communicated, understood, and reviewed on a scheduled basis.

Any breach of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including
termination of employment or contract.

================================================================================

2. USER ACCESS MANAGEMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Access to organisational systems and information assets is granted strictly on a
need-to-know and need-to-use basis, consistent with the principle of least
privilege. Access rights are determined by an individual's role and
responsibilities within the Organisation.

The process for granting, modifying, and revoking access rights is as follows:

  a) Formal Access Request: All new access requests must be submitted via the
     IT Service Desk portal using the Access Request Form (ARF). Requests must
     include a business justification and be approved by the requesting
     employee's line manager and the system owner.

  b) Provisioning: Upon approval, the IT team provisions access rights in
     accordance with the approved role profile. Access is restricted to the
     minimum permissions required to perform defined job functions.

  c) Periodic Review: Access rights for all users are reviewed on a quarterly
     basis by system owners and line managers. Any access no longer required
     must be revoked within five business days of the review.

  d) Access Restriction: Users are only permitted to access information and
     systems authorised for their role. Sharing of credentials, access cards,
     or authentication tokens is strictly prohibited. Role-Based Access Control
     (RBAC) is applied across all critical systems to enforce this separation.

================================================================================

3. PRIVILEGED ACCESS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Administrative and privileged access rights represent the highest-risk access
category and are subject to additional controls beyond standard user access
management.

  a) Minimum Grant: Privileged access is granted only when operationally
     necessary and only for the specific system or environment requiring
     elevation. The principle of least privilege is strictly enforced.
     Administrative accounts are separate from standard user accounts.

  b) Privileged Account Registry: All privileged accounts — including local
     administrator, domain administrator, database administrator (DBA), and
     superuser (root) accounts — must be registered in the Privileged Access
     Management (PAM) system. The use of shared or generic administrative
     accounts is prohibited.

  c) Monitoring and Logging: All privileged access sessions are logged and
     subject to real-time monitoring. Logs are retained for a minimum of twelve
     months. Any anomalous privileged activity triggers an automated alert to
     the Security Operations Centre (SOC).

  d) Time-Limited Access: Privileged access may be granted on a just-in-time
     (JIT) basis for specific maintenance windows. Such access expires
     automatically at the end of the approved window.

================================================================================

4. REMOTE ACCESS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Remote access to organisational systems, networks, and data is permitted only
under the following conditions:

  a) VPN Requirement: All remote connections to internal systems must be
     established through the Organisation's approved Virtual Private Network
     (VPN) solution. Direct remote desktop connections to internal servers
     without VPN are prohibited.

  b) Secure Authentication: Remote access sessions require secure authentication
     using Organisation-approved credentials. Session inactivity timeout is set
     to 15 minutes, after which the session is automatically terminated and
     re-authentication is required.

  c) Device Compliance: Only Organisation-managed or approved personal devices
     configured with endpoint security controls (antivirus, disk encryption, OS
     patching) may be used to establish remote connections.

  d) Prohibited Uses: Remote access must not be used to transfer confidential
     data to unapproved personal storage, to circumvent network security
     controls, or to allow third-party access without explicit authorisation.

  e) Network Security: Remote connections traverse encrypted network channels.
     Firewall rules restrict remote access to only the services and systems
     required by each user's role.

================================================================================

5. ACCESS REVIEW AND TERMINATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  a) Periodic Access Reviews: All user access rights are reviewed at minimum
     every six months by the relevant system owner and line manager. The review
     must confirm continued business justification for each access grant.
     Results are documented and retained for audit purposes.

  b) Immediate Revocation on Termination: Upon termination of employment or
     contract — whether voluntary or involuntary — all logical access rights,
     physical access tokens, VPN certificates, and system credentials are
     revoked within twenty-four (24) hours of the termination notification.

  c) Role Change: When an employee changes roles, all access rights associated
     with the former role are revoked and new rights appropriate to the new role
     are provisioned through the standard access request process. Cumulative
     access rights that exceed the new role's requirements must be removed.

  d) Return of Assets: Upon termination, the employee must return all
     Organisation-issued equipment, access cards, and authentication tokens
     in accordance with the Organisation's exit procedures.
